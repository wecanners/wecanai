Dumped, Replaced & Trending: The #QuitGPT Moment

Published 2 March 2026 · By Joziane El Hawi

When a large institution changes AI provider, it rarely stays a procurement footnote. It becomes a narrative. The episode that trended as #QuitGPT is a useful example of how quickly a contracting decision turns into a public story, and how much of that story is signal versus noise.

What actually happened

At its core this was a story about provider choice: which AI vendor an organization relies on, what it costs to switch, and what switching signals to everyone watching. The headlines framed it as a dramatic breakup. The reality, as usual, was more about contracts, capabilities and risk tolerance than about drama.

The lesson for your team

Provider choice is a real strategic decision, and lock-in is a real cost. A team that understands the AI tool landscape, rather than depending on one assistant by habit, is a team that can switch deliberately instead of being switched by events. Tool selection is one of the core literacy skills we teach.
