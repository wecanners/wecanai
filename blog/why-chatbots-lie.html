Why Chatbots Lie: The Truth About Hallucinations

Published 31 January 2026 · By Joziane El Hawi

It is a curious thing to watch the AI industry publish research that, almost in passing, admits how much of its foundation is still uncertain.

Work on why language models hallucinate is usually framed as clarity on a technical nuisance: why chatbots sometimes state false things with full confidence. Read closely, it delivers something more interesting, a clearer view of how these systems actually work, and where they do not.

What a hallucination really is

A hallucination is not a glitch in the ordinary sense. It is the model doing exactly what it was built to do, predicting plausible text, in a situation where plausible and true have come apart. The model has no separate sense of "I know this" versus "I am guessing." It produces fluent output either way.

The practical takeaway

This is why verification is not an optional extra in responsible AI use. It is the core skill. A confident answer is a starting point for checking, never the end of the process. We build that habit into every workflow we teach.
