Our Lawless AI Era: Technology Outpaces Wisdom

Published 14 February 2026 · By Joziane El Hawi

It took more than a decade, hundreds of deaths, thousands of injuries and substantial economic damage before society recognized that teaching people to drive was not optional. It was essential for public safety.

Today's AI development mirrors the chaotic early automobile era in several critical ways. Powerful tools are widely available. Training and norms lag far behind access. And the cost of that gap is paid in mistakes that were avoidable.

The driving analogy, taken seriously

This is the analogy at the heart of how we think about AI. You would not hand someone the keys to a car and say "good luck." Driving requires rules, training and responsible habits. AI works the same way. The technology is not the problem; the absence of an operating standard is.

Closing the gap

The good news is that we do not have to wait a decade this time. Organizations can choose now to give their teams the equivalent of driver training: literacy, rules and practiced habits. That choice is what separates confident adoption from costly improvisation.
